Gas vs Electric Furnace: Technical Fuel Comparison

Detailed technical comparison of gas and electric furnaces, including combustion vs. resistance heating, efficiency metrics, operating characteristics, installation requirements, and cost optimization strategies.

Photo by [Photographer] on Unsplash - Gas vs electric furnace technical analysis

Fuel Source Fundamentals

Gas Furnace Technology

Combustion-based heating using natural gas or propane. Heat exchanger transfers combustion heat to air stream. Requires venting for exhaust gases. Efficiency measured by AFUE (Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency).

Electric Furnace Technology

Resistance heating using electrical coils. 100% electrical energy conversion to heat. No combustion, no venting required. Efficiency measured by COP (Coefficient of Performance), typically 1.0 for resistance heat.

Efficiency Analysis

Heating Efficiency

  • Gas Furnace: AFUE 80-97% (80% minimum, 97% condensing maximum)
  • Electric Furnace: 100% electrical efficiency, 95-98% overall system efficiency

Energy Source Comparison

  • Natural Gas: ~1,000 BTU/cubic foot, $1.00-$1.50/therm
  • Electricity: 3,412 BTU/kWh, $0.12-$0.15/kWh average
  • Cost Ratio: Gas typically 2-3 times cheaper per BTU

Seasonal Performance

Gas furnaces maintain consistent efficiency across temperature ranges. Electric furnaces have COP = 1.0 regardless of outdoor conditions. Gas systems more cost-effective in cold climates.

Operating Characteristics

Heating Capacity

  • Gas Furnace: 40,000-120,000 BTU/hr capacity range
  • Electric Furnace: 10-25 kW (34,000-85,000 BTU/hr)

Response Time

  • Gas Furnace: 30-60 seconds from call to heat delivery
  • Electric Furnace: Instant heat delivery, 10-30 seconds

Air Quality

  • Gas Furnace: Potential CO production, requires ventilation
  • Electric Furnace: No combustion products, clean air heating

Installation Requirements

Utility Connections

  • Gas Furnace: Gas line connection, electrical service, venting
  • Electric Furnace: Electrical service only, no gas or venting

Space Requirements

  • Gas Furnace: 3x3 ft footprint, clearance for service access
  • Electric Furnace: Similar footprint, no venting clearance needed

Building Modifications

  • Gas Furnace: Vent termination through roof/wall, gas piping
  • Electric Furnace: May require electrical panel upgrade

Cost Analysis

Initial Investment

  • Gas Furnace: $4,000-$8,000 installed (AFUE 95%)
  • Electric Furnace: $2,500-$5,000 installed (high-efficiency)

Operating Costs

  • Gas Furnace: Lower monthly costs in most regions
  • Electric Furnace: Higher operating costs, 2-3x gas equivalent

Maintenance Costs

  • Gas Furnace: Annual tune-up $75-$150, higher maintenance
  • Electric Furnace: Minimal maintenance, $50-$100 annually

Reliability Factors

System Longevity

  • Gas Furnace: 15-20 years heat exchanger, 20-30 years cabinet
  • Electric Furnace: 20-30 years electrical components, longer life

Failure Modes

  • Gas Furnace: Heat exchanger cracks, burner failure, gas valve issues
  • Electric Furnace: Element failure, sequencer problems, electrical issues

Environmental Impact

Carbon Emissions

  • Gas Furnace: ~117 lbs CO2/MMBtu natural gas combustion
  • Electric Furnace: Depends on grid mix, ~50-80% cleaner than gas

Indoor Air Quality

  • Gas Furnace: Potential for CO, NOx, particulate emissions
  • Electric Furnace: No combustion emissions, cleaner indoor air

Selection Criteria

Fuel Availability

  • Choose Gas: Natural gas infrastructure available, high heating loads
  • Choose Electric: No gas service, moderate loads, clean energy preference

Cost Optimization

  • Gas Furnace: Lower long-term costs in cold climates
  • Electric Furnace: Consider time-of-use rates, renewable energy credits

Portland-Specific Considerations

Natural gas widely available in Portland metro area. PGE offers clean energy options that improve electric furnace environmental profile. Consider future electrification trends and utility incentives.

Building codes require sealed combustion for new gas installations. Seismic bracing affects both systems. Marine climate corrosion impacts outdoor components.
