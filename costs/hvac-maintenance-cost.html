HVAC Maintenance Cost

Regular HVAC maintenance prevents costly repairs and ensures efficient operation. Understanding typical costs helps homeowners budget for essential services in Portland's variable climate.

Photo by 404 on Unsplash - Cost planning tools

Annual Tune-Up Costs

Professional annual maintenance inspections cost $75-$150 for standard systems. Comprehensive tune-ups include cleaning, inspection, and adjustments.

Labor costs range from $50-$100 per hour, with inspections taking 1-2 hours. Additional costs may apply for extensive cleaning or minor repairs.

Filter Replacement Costs

Standard furnace filters cost $5-$15 each, with HVAC-grade filters ranging $10-$30. Replacement frequency depends on usage and air quality.

Professional filter replacement services cost $25-$50 per visit. DIY replacement saves money but requires proper sizing and installation.

Coil Cleaning Costs

Professional evaporator coil cleaning costs $100-$300, depending on accessibility and contamination level. Condenser coil cleaning ranges $50-$150.

Chemical treatments add $50-$100 to cleaning costs. Severe contamination may require coil replacement at $500-$1,500.

Drain Line Maintenance

Condensate drain line cleaning costs $75-$200 per visit. Annual maintenance prevents clogs and water damage.

Professional cleaning includes inspection, chemical treatment, and flushing. UV light installations for mold prevention cost $200-$500.

Refrigerant Checks

Refrigerant level testing costs $75-$150 during tune-ups. Recharging costs $150-$400 per pound of refrigerant plus labor.

Environmental regulations require certified technicians for refrigerant handling. Leaks require repair before recharging.

Electrical Component Testing

Control board and electrical component testing costs $100-$300. Capacitor replacement costs $150-$400 including labor.

Thermostat calibration or replacement costs $100-$300. Smart thermostat installation adds $200-$500 to basic service.

Maintenance Contracts

Annual maintenance contracts cost $150-$400 per year for standard coverage. Contracts include priority scheduling and discounts on repairs.

Extended warranties cost $300-$800 annually, covering major component failures. Contracts provide predictable budgeting for homeowners.

DIY Maintenance Savings

Filter changes and basic cleaning save $50-$200 annually. Visual inspections and thermostat checks require no professional cost.

However, complex maintenance like coil cleaning or electrical testing should always be professional to avoid damage or safety risks.
