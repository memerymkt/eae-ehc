HVAC Energy Audit: Technical Assessment

Comprehensive technical energy audit procedures for HVAC systems, including efficiency analysis, performance testing, cost savings calculations, and optimization recommendations for improved operation.

Photo by [Photographer] on Unsplash - HVAC energy audit and technical assessment

Audit Preparation

System Documentation

  • Gather equipment manuals and specifications
  • Review installation records and maintenance history
  • Document system configuration and components
  • Collect utility bills for the past 12-24 months
  • Identify system age and major modifications

Measurement Tools

  • Digital multimeter for electrical measurements
  • Manifold gauges for refrigerant pressures
  • Anemometer for airflow velocity
  • Infrared thermometer for temperature readings
  • Psychrometer for humidity measurements
  • Power analyzer for energy consumption

Visual Inspection

Equipment Condition

  • Check for visible damage or corrosion
  • Inspect electrical connections and wiring
  • Examine refrigerant lines for leaks or damage
  • Verify proper clearances and ventilation
  • Check for unusual noises or vibrations

Ductwork Assessment

  • Inspect for air leaks and disconnected joints
  • Check duct insulation condition
  • Verify proper sizing and routing
  • Examine register and grille operation
  • Look for signs of moisture or mold

Performance Testing

Efficiency Measurements

  • Calculate actual SEER vs. rated SEER
  • Measure AFUE for gas furnaces
  • Test HSPF for heat pumps
  • Determine part-load efficiency
  • Compare to industry standards

Airflow Analysis

  • Measure supply air CFM at registers
  • Check return air velocities
  • Calculate total system airflow
  • Verify proper air balancing
  • Identify restrictions and blockages

Temperature Analysis

  • Measure temperature rise/drop across equipment
  • Check superheat and subcooling
  • Verify thermostat calibration
  • Assess zone temperature variations
  • Monitor for short cycling

Energy Consumption Analysis

Load Calculations

  • Perform Manual J load calculation
  • Compare actual load to system capacity
  • Identify oversizing or undersizing issues
  • Assess building envelope efficiency
  • Calculate optimum system sizing

Operating Cost Assessment

  • Calculate annual energy consumption
  • Determine peak demand charges
  • Assess time-of-use rate impacts
  • Compare fuel type efficiency
  • Project future energy costs

Diagnostic Procedures

Refrigerant Circuit Analysis

  • Measure suction and discharge pressures
  • Calculate superheat and subcooling
  • Check for refrigerant leaks
  • Verify proper charge levels
  • Test expansion valve operation

Electrical System Testing

  • Measure voltage and amperage
  • Check power factor and harmonics
  • Test capacitor values
  • Verify motor efficiency
  • Assess control system operation

Recommendations Development

Priority Classifications

  • High Priority: Safety issues, major efficiency losses
  • Medium Priority: Performance improvements, moderate savings
  • Low Priority: Minor optimizations, future considerations

Cost-Benefit Analysis

  • Calculate simple payback periods
  • Determine net present value
  • Assess return on investment
  • Consider available incentives
  • Project long-term savings

Implementation Plan

  • Prioritize recommendations by impact
  • Develop phased implementation schedule
  • Identify qualified contractors
  • Establish monitoring and verification
  • Create maintenance schedule

Reporting and Documentation

Audit Report Structure

  • Executive summary with key findings
  • Detailed system assessment
  • Performance test results
  • Energy savings calculations
  • Recommendations with priorities
  • Implementation cost estimates

Measurement and Verification

  • Establish baseline performance metrics
  • Define post-implementation monitoring
  • Set up automated data collection
  • Schedule follow-up assessments
  • Document achieved savings

Portland-Specific Considerations

Portland's moderate climate affects HVAC efficiency calculations. Utility programs provide incentives for audits. Building codes require energy efficiency compliance. Marine air impacts equipment performance. Local contractors offer audit services.
