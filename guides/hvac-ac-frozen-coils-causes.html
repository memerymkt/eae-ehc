HVAC AC Frozen Coils Causes: Why It Happens and How to Fix in Portland

Frozen AC coils are a common problem that can severely impact your air conditioner's performance and efficiency. Understanding the causes and solutions helps Portland homeowners prevent damage and maintain reliable cooling. This guide covers everything you need to know about frozen evaporator coils.

What Are Frozen AC Coils?

Frozen coils occur when ice forms on the evaporator coil inside your air handler. The evaporator coil cools and dehumidifies air as it passes through. When moisture condenses on the cold coil and freezes, it creates ice buildup that blocks airflow and reduces cooling efficiency.

Frozen coils are typically the evaporator coil, not the outdoor condenser coil.

Signs of Frozen AC Coils

  • Reduced cooling performance or warm air blowing
  • AC runs constantly without reaching desired temperature
  • Ice visible on indoor unit or refrigerant lines
  • Hissing or bubbling sounds from the unit
  • Increased energy bills
  • Water leaking from the unit

These symptoms indicate airflow restriction and potential damage if not addressed.

Top Causes of Frozen AC Coils

  • Dirty Air Filter: Clogged filter restricts airflow, causing coil to get too cold and freeze
  • Dirty Evaporator Coil: Dust and debris insulate coil, preventing proper heat exchange
  • Blocked Return Vents: Obstructed vents reduce airflow over the coil
  • Faulty Thermostat: Incorrect readings cause AC to run too long or not shut off
  • Low Refrigerant: Insufficient refrigerant causes coil to get too cold

In Portland's humid climate, dirty filters and coils are particularly common.

How to Fix Frozen AC Coils

  • Turn Off the AC: Switch thermostat to Off to allow natural thawing
  • Change Air Filter: Replace with clean filter to restore airflow
  • Allow Thawing: Let ice melt completely (may take hours)
  • Check Vents: Ensure all return vents are clear
  • Test Thermostat: Verify proper operation

Prevention Tips

  • Change air filters monthly during cooling season
  • Schedule annual AC maintenance and cleaning
  • Keep return vents clear and unobstructed
  • Test thermostat accuracy annually
  • Ensure proper refrigerant levels

Regular maintenance prevents most freezing issues in Portland homes.

When to Call a Professional

Contact an HVAC technician if:

  • Coils freeze repeatedly
  • You suspect refrigerant issues
  • AC shows other performance problems
  • Ice doesn't thaw after 24 hours
  • You're uncomfortable diagnosing the issue

Professional service ensures safe repairs and identifies underlying problems.
