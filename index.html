Why Indoor Air Quality Matters

The average person spends approximately 90% of their time indoors, making indoor air quality a critical factor in overall health and comfort. EPA studies consistently show that indoor air can be 2-5 times more polluted than outdoor air, with some indoor environments containing pollutant levels up to 100 times higher than outdoor concentrations.

Common indoor air quality issues include:

  • Particulate Matter: Dust, pollen, pet dander, and smoke particles that trigger respiratory symptoms
  • Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs): Chemical emissions from furniture, cleaning products, and building materials
  • Biological Contaminants: Mold spores, bacteria, and viruses that thrive in poorly managed indoor environments
  • Humidity Imbalances: Too much or too little moisture creating discomfort and structural issues
  • Inadequate Ventilation: Insufficient fresh air exchange leading to stale, polluted indoor air

Pacific Northwest Climate Considerations

Portland's unique climate presents specific challenges for maintaining optimal indoor air quality and comfort. Our mild, wet winters and dry summers create seasonal variations in humidity levels, while the region's excellent outdoor air quality makes proper ventilation strategies particularly beneficial.

During Portland's rainy season, elevated outdoor humidity can contribute to indoor moisture problems without proper humidity control. Conversely, dry summer months may require humidification to maintain comfortable indoor conditions. Understanding these seasonal patterns helps homeowners select appropriate equipment and develop effective maintenance schedules.
