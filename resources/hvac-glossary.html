HVAC Glossary: Technical Terminology

Comprehensive glossary of HVAC technical terms, acronyms, and definitions for professional understanding, system design, and optimization in residential and commercial applications.

Photo by [Photographer] on Unsplash - HVAC technical glossary and reference

A

AFUE (Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency)

Measure of gas furnace heating efficiency over a full heating season. Calculated as annual output BTU divided by annual input BTU, expressed as a percentage.

Air Handler

Indoor component of split-system HVAC that contains the blower, evaporator coil, and air filters. Circulates conditioned air through ductwork.

Ambient Temperature

Temperature of the surrounding environment. Used as reference for system design and performance calculations.

B

BTU (British Thermal Unit)

Unit of heat energy. One BTU equals the heat required to raise one pound of water one degree Fahrenheit. Used to measure heating and cooling capacity.

Blower Motor

Electric motor that drives the fan in air handlers and furnaces. Available in single-speed, multi-speed, and variable-speed configurations.

By-pass Humidifier

Humidification system that uses furnace blower to distribute moisture. Water flows through a pad and evaporates into the air stream.

C

CFM (Cubic Feet per Minute)

Measure of airflow volume. Standard unit for expressing fan capacity and ductwork sizing. Calculated as velocity × area × 1440 / 60.

Compressor

Heart of the refrigeration system. Compresses refrigerant vapor, raising its temperature and pressure. Types include reciprocating, scroll, and rotary.

Condenser Coil

Outdoor heat exchanger where refrigerant releases heat to the outside air. Part of the condensing unit in split systems.

COP (Coefficient of Performance)

Ratio of heating or cooling provided to electrical energy consumed. Higher COP indicates more efficient operation.

D

Damper

Device for controlling airflow in ductwork. Manual or motorized dampers used for zoning and airflow balancing.

Defrost Cycle

Process in heat pumps where frost is removed from outdoor coil. Uses reverse valve to melt ice accumulation.

Dry Bulb Temperature

Air temperature measured by a standard thermometer. Used in psychrometric calculations and system design.

E

EER (Energy Efficiency Ratio)

Measure of air conditioner efficiency at peak load conditions. Calculated as BTU/hr divided by watts. Lower than SEER for same system.

ESP (External Static Pressure)

Resistance to airflow created by ductwork, filters, and grilles. Measured in inches of water column. Affects blower performance.

Evaporator Coil

Indoor heat exchanger where refrigerant absorbs heat from indoor air. Located in air handler or furnace.

F

Fan Coil Unit

Terminal unit with fan, coil, and filter. Used in central systems or as standalone units in multi-zone applications.

Flue

Vent pipe that carries combustion products to the outside. Must be properly sized and terminated according to code.

G

Gas Valve

Controls gas flow to furnace burners. Multi-stage valves provide modulating heat output. Includes safety shutoff features.

Grains per Pound

Unit for measuring moisture content in air. One grain equals 1/7000 pound. Used in humidity calculations.

H

Heat Exchanger

Device for transferring heat between two fluids. Primary heat exchanger in furnaces transfers heat from combustion gases to air.

HSPF (Heating Seasonal Performance Factor)

Measure of heat pump heating efficiency over a full heating season. Higher HSPF indicates more efficient heating operation.

Humidistat

Device that measures relative humidity and controls humidification or dehumidification equipment.

L

Latent Heat

Heat energy associated with phase changes of water. Used in dehumidification and humidification calculations.

Line Set

Copper tubing connecting indoor and outdoor units in split systems. Consists of liquid line and suction line.

M

Manual J

ACC A method for calculating residential heating and cooling loads. Considers building envelope, orientation, and internal gains.

MERV Rating

Minimum Efficiency Reporting Value for air filters. Higher MERV indicates better filtration of small particles.

P

Psychrometric Chart

Graph showing thermodynamic properties of moist air. Used for system design and troubleshooting.

Purge

Process of removing air and moisture from refrigeration system during charging or repair.

R

R-410A

HFC refrigerant used in modern air conditioning systems. Higher pressure than R-22, requires different equipment.

Refrigerant

Working fluid in vapor compression systems. Absorbs heat in evaporator, releases heat in condenser.

Return Air

Air drawn back to the HVAC system for conditioning. Must be properly filtered and distributed.

S

SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio)

Measure of air conditioner efficiency over a full cooling season. Weighted average of performance at different temperatures.

Sensible Heat

Heat energy that changes air temperature without changing moisture content. Used in cooling load calculations.

Subcooling

Amount refrigerant is cooled below its saturation temperature in liquid form. Indicator of proper charge and system performance.

Superheat

Amount refrigerant is heated above its saturation temperature in vapor form. Used to verify proper evaporator performance.

T

Thermostat

Control device that senses room temperature and signals HVAC equipment to maintain setpoint.

Ton

Unit of cooling capacity. One ton equals 12,000 BTU/hr. Based on heat required to melt one ton of ice in 24 hours.

V

Vapor Compression Cycle

Refrigeration process involving compression, condensation, expansion, and evaporation of refrigerant.

Venturi Effect

Principle used in gas valves and orifices. Pressure difference creates flow through restriction.

W

Wet Bulb Temperature

Temperature measured by thermometer with wet wick. Indicates moisture content of air. Used in psychrometric calculations.

Portland-Specific Terms

Marine Climate

Coastal weather pattern with high humidity and moderate temperatures. Affects equipment selection and corrosion protection.

Seismic Zone 3

Earthquake risk category for Portland area. Requires special bracing and anchoring for HVAC equipment.

Energy Trust

Oregon utility program providing rebates for energy-efficient HVAC installations and upgrades.
