HVAC Maintenance Checklist: Technical Protocols

Comprehensive HVAC maintenance checklist with technical protocols, detailed inspection procedures, and preventive maintenance schedules to ensure system reliability and optimal performance.

Photo by [Photographer] on Unsplash - HVAC maintenance checklist and protocols

Monthly Maintenance Tasks

Filter Inspection and Replacement

  • Visual inspection for dust accumulation and damage
  • Pressure drop measurement: <0.3" w.c. clean
  • Filter replacement schedule: every 1-3 months based on usage
  • MERV rating verification: appropriate for air quality needs
  • Housing seal check: no bypass air leaks

Thermostat and Control System

  • Battery level check: replace if <2.0V
  • Display test: all segments functioning
  • Temperature accuracy: ±1°F calibration
  • Program verification: correct time and schedules
  • Remote access test: app connectivity if applicable

Visual System Inspection

  • Unusual noises: vibration or grinding sounds
  • Odor detection: musty smells or gas leaks
  • Airflow observation: consistent register output
  • Condensate drainage: clear and unobstructed
  • Outdoor unit clearance: 2' minimum around perimeter

Quarterly Maintenance Tasks

Air Distribution System

  • Register cleaning: remove dust and debris
  • Damper operation: full open/close functionality
  • Duct inspection: visible cracks or separation
  • Airflow balancing: ±10% between zones
  • Return air grille: unobstructed intake

Electrical Components

  • Connection tightness: torque to manufacturer specs
  • Wire insulation: no cracks or exposed conductors
  • Capacitor test: within 10% of rated capacitance
  • Contactor contacts: clean and not pitted
  • Ground continuity: <1 ohm resistance

Refrigeration Circuit

  • Pressure gauge readings: compare to design conditions
  • Temperature measurements: superheat and subcooling
  • Refrigerant level: visual sight glass if equipped
  • Line insulation: intact and properly secured
  • Leak detection: electronic sniffer test

Annual Maintenance Tasks

Combustion System (Gas Furnaces)

  • Heat exchanger inspection: cracks or corrosion
  • Burner orifice cleaning: no debris accumulation
  • Gas valve calibration: manifold pressure 3.5" w.c.
  • Flame sensor cleaning: continuity test
  • Venting system: proper draft and termination
  • Carbon monoxide test: <50 ppm ambient

Air Handling Unit

  • Blower wheel cleaning: remove dust buildup
  • Motor bearing lubrication: if applicable
  • Belt tension: deflection test
  • Drain pan cleaning: antimicrobial treatment
  • Coil inspection: fin damage or fouling
  • Airflow measurement: anemometer verification

Condensing Unit

  • Coil cleaning: low-pressure rinse
  • Fan motor lubrication: sealed bearings check
  • Compressor operation: amp draw test
  • Refrigerant charge: recovery and recharge
  • Electrical connections: corrosion inspection
  • Vibration isolation: mounts integrity

Bi-Annual Maintenance Tasks

Summer Service (Pre-Season)

  • System startup test: full cooling cycle
  • Refrigerant analysis: leak check and charge
  • Electrical system: voltage and amperage
  • Airflow verification: design CFM
  • Thermostat calibration: temperature accuracy

Winter Service (Pre-Season)

  • Heating cycle test: full operation
  • Combustion analysis: CO2 and CO levels
  • Limit switch verification: temperature settings
  • Ductwork sealing: air leakage test
  • Humidifier preparation: pad replacement

Technical Inspection Protocols

Performance Testing

  • Efficiency measurement: SEER/AFUE verification
  • Capacity test: BTU output measurement
  • Static pressure: ESP readings
  • Temperature rise/fall: design verification
  • Power consumption: kWh monitoring

Safety Control Verification

  • High limit switches: temperature calibration
  • Pressure switches: cut-in/cut-out settings
  • Flame safeguards: lockout timing
  • Emergency shutdown: manual reset functionality
  • Interlock systems: proper sequencing

Documentation and Records

Maintenance Log

  • Date and technician identification
  • Procedures performed and results
  • Parts replaced and reasons
  • Performance measurements
  • Recommendations for future service

Warranty Compliance

  • Manufacturer requirements: service intervals
  • Documentation retention: 5-10 years
  • Authorized technician verification
  • Parts traceability: OEM components

Portland-Specific Considerations

Portland's climate requires enhanced maintenance for humidity control and corrosion prevention. Schedule seasonal service before weather extremes. Document all maintenance for insurance and warranty purposes.
